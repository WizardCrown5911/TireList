How to Make a Tier List

A tier list ranks items into labelled groups — usually S, A, B, C, and D — from best to worst or strongest to weakest. They are used to rank video game characters, films, albums, foods, athletes, and anything else you want to sort by preference or quality. This guide explains how to build one from scratch using Forge TierList.

Open the builder → About Forge TierList

Step-by-step guide

Step 1

Set a list title

Open the builder and type your topic into the List title field in the Control Bay on the left. A specific title works better than a vague one. "Best Soulslike Games 2024" gives the item finder more to work with than "games". If your items belong to a specific category, add that in the Context field below the title.

Step 2

Add items to the pool

Click Find from title to auto-generate a list of related items based on your title. You will see a suggestion panel with checkboxes — select the items you want and click Add selected or Add all. You can also type your own items manually in the text area below, one item per line. Optional context can follow the name: Mario | Nintendo character or Mario - Nintendo. All new items start with auto-generated text cards showing the item name.

Step 3

Drag items into tiers

Items arrive in the Pool at the bottom of the board. Drag a card up and drop it into any tier row — S, A, B, C, or D. Drag it again to move it to a different tier or to reorder it within the same tier. To add a new tier, click Add tier at the bottom of the board. To remove a tier, open the tier's menu and click Remove — all items return to the pool.

Step 4

Add images to cards

Click the menu button on any card to open image options. Choose Search image to find a public domain or Creative Commons image from Wikimedia Commons or Openverse. If several candidates are found, a picker lets you choose the best match. You can also Upload image to use your own file, or keep the auto-generated text card if no image is needed. To replace all text cards at once, use Search all images from the board toolbar.

Step 5

Customise tiers and themes

Click a tier label to rename it. Click the colour swatch next to the label to change the tier colour. The theme menu in the toolbar offers preset colour palettes — Forge (default), Aurora, Inferno, and Toxic — or switch to Custom to edit each tier colour individually. Compact mode reduces card size so more items fit on screen at once.

Step 6

Export or save your tier list

Click Export PNG in the toolbar to download a high-resolution image of the board without the pool. The file is named after your list title. To save to your personal dashboard, sign in with Google and click Save to dashboard. Saved lists are synced across devices. You can also export the list as a JSON file from the toolbar to keep a local backup or share it with someone using the same app.

Tips for better tier lists

  • Use a specific title. The more specific the title, the better the auto-generated item suggestions will be.
  • Add context for image matching. The context field helps the image search find the right pictures when item names are ambiguous — for example, "Mercury" matches the planet rather than the car brand if the context is "solar system".
  • Work in compact mode for large lists. Toggle compact mode to fit more cards on screen at once when ranking 20 or more items.
  • Use JSON export for backups. If you are not signed in, export the list as JSON before closing the tab to avoid losing your work.
  • Reset placements without clearing items. The Reset placements button moves all cards back to the pool but keeps your items, so you can start the ranking from scratch without re-entering everything.

What can you rank with a tier list?

Tier lists work for almost any set of items where you have preferences or quality judgements. Popular topics include:

  • Video game characters, weapons, maps, or levels
  • Films, TV series, or episodes within a series
  • Music albums, songs, or artists within a genre
  • Foods, ingredients, or restaurants
  • Sports players, teams, or kits
  • Books, manga, or comic story arcs
  • Programming languages, frameworks, or tools
  • Anything else you want to sort from best to worst

Frequently asked questions

Is Forge TierList free?

Yes. Building, exporting, and importing tier lists is free with no account required. Sign in with Google to enable cloud saving.

Do I need to create an account?

No. You can build and export tier lists without signing in. Sign in with Google only if you want to save lists to a personal dashboard and access them on other devices.

Can I use my own images?

Yes. Click the menu on any card and choose Upload image to use a PNG, JPEG, WebP, GIF, or AVIF file from your device.

How do I share a tier list?

Export the board as a PNG and share the image directly. You can also export the list as a JSON file which another person can import into Forge TierList to see or edit your ranking.

Can I add more than 5 tiers?

Yes. Click Add tier at the bottom of the board to create as many tiers as you need. You can also remove any tier by clicking its menu button.
